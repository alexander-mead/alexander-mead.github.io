About Me

My name is Alexander Mead and I am a cosmologist currently holding a CITA post-doctoral fellowship and working with Ludo van Waerbeke at the University of British Columbia in Vancouver on topics relating to weak gravitational lensing. I completed an undergraduate-Masters degree at The University of Oxford and worked on projects to do with mass transfer between stars in binary systems. I worked on my PhD at the University of Edinburgh under the supervision of John Peacock and also did a short post-doc position with Catherine Heymans. My main interest is how non-linear cosmological structure formation is influenced by 'beyond-standard-cosmological-model' physics, such as baryonic feedback, massive neutrinos, dark energy and modified gravitational physics.

Science

I am interested in how non-linear structure formation can be understood using inspiration from cosmological n-body simulations. These simulations are extremely useful, but are too expensive to be run for every possible cosmological model under consideration. I have worked on 'rescaling' methods to alter the cosmology of a previously run simulation by remapping length and time units and then modifying halo structure. More recently, I have looked at using the halo model to produce accurate matter power spectra, which may be useful for analyising weak lensing data sets; the publicly available HMcode is the result of this work and provides non-linear spectra. It is also incorporated within CAMB.
