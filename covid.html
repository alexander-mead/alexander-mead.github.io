COVID-19

COVID-19 cases, hospital admissions and deaths in the four nations of the UK. Note that the definition of hospital admission differs between the four nations, so these data are not directly comparable. Wales is particularly different to the other nations as both confirmed and suspected COVID cases are included. This is explained here. Data from gov.uk.

COVID-19 cases and deaths broken down by region of the UK. English regional data from gov.uk.

COVID-19 deaths compared to typical flu deaths over the course of the pandemic. Shown are flu deaths in a typical year, averaged over the whole year, and flu deaths in a 'bad' year averaged over the worst three months of the flu season. For comparison, also shown is the typical number of deaths the UK experiences in an average day of an average year (although note that this varies seasonally, with more deaths in winter). UK data from Johns Hopkins University.
